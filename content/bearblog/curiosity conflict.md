In my opinion, shifting from exploration to exploitation is pretty much like shifting from consuming to creating.

Curiosity brings dopamine, dopamine brings motivation on exploring all possibilities, but not the more _focused work of exploitation_. This is called [Curiosity Conflict](https://nesslabs.com/curiosity-conflict).

To resolve curiosity conflict, I can:

- keep a curiosity inbox - put everything interesting in it (new ideas), manage the rating as time goes on, get a ranked list of ideas based on my interests
- [Timeshielding: How to Do the Things you Actually Want to Do](https://nesslabs.com/timeshielding-time-management)
- find partners
- practice metacognition - try [plus minus next](https://nesslabs.com/plus-minus-next) sheet and review weekly to find out what is worth focusing on
- embrace [JOMO](https://nesslabs.com/jomo): the joy of missing out (comparing to FOMO, the fear of missing out)

These are a collection of Anne-Laure Le Cunff's ideas. They are valuable and work for me. I would like to note them down and share them with people.
